## 📝 Writing in Scrivener

The interface consists of three main parts. The Binder sits on the left side of the screen. This area lists every chapter and note in your project. Click any item in the Binder to open it in the main editor. 

The main editor occupies the center of the screen. This is where you type your text. You can change fonts and styles using the bar at the top of the editor.

The Inspector sits on the right side. Use this space for notes. You can write summaries of your scenes here. You can also attach pictures or documents to your scenes.

## 📂 Managing your research

You might gather images, web links, or PDF files while you write. Store these items in the Research folder inside your Binder. Scrivener keeps these files next to your writing project. You never have to switch between windows to look at your notes. Drag and drop any file from your computer into the Research folder to save it.

## 📤 Exporting your work

You must turn your project into a standard document for others to read. Scrivener calls this process compiling.

1. Click File.
2. Select Compile.
3. Choose the format from the list. Choose PDF for simple documents. Choose Word if you need to edit the text in another program.
4. Select the chapters you want to include in the final export.
5. Click the Compile button.
6. The software builds a single document from your individual sections.
